Inside the event horizon

Jan 2025 · Black holes

Black holes stretch intuition: time slows, spacetime curves, and signals cannot escape beyond the event horizon. I collected a few notes on how energy, mass, and information play together near that boundary.

One handy relation: the Schwarzschild radius scales with mass, setting the size of the point of no return.

Inside you can imagine the region bounded by the event horizon; we tie the radius of that boundary to the mass in the equation that follows.

Schwarzschild radius \( r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2} \)
